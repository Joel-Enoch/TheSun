Welcome to Our Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of our Solar System. It provides the energy that supports all life on Earth and governs the climate and weather. Learn about the structure, fascinating phenomena, and more!

Structure of the Sun

The Sun is made up of several layers:

  • Core: The center where nuclear fusion occurs.
  • Radiative Zone: Energy slowly moves outward here.
  • Convective Zone: Hot gases rise and cool gases sink.
  • Photosphere: The visible surface of the Sun.
  • Chromosphere: The layer above the photosphere.
  • Corona: The outermost, hot plasma layer.

Solar Phenomena

The Sun exhibits various fascinating phenomena, including:

  • Sunspots: Darker, cooler areas on the Sun's surface.
  • Solar Flares: Sudden, intense bursts of energy.
  • Coronal Mass Ejections (CMEs): Huge bursts of solar wind and magnetic fields.
  • Solar Wind: Streams of charged particles released from the corona.

Fun Facts About the Sun

  • The Sun is about 4.6 billion years old.
  • It is 93 million miles (150 million km) away from Earth.
  • The Sun's diameter is about 109 times that of Earth.
  • It takes sunlight about 8 minutes to reach Earth.
